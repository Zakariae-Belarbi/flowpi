FlowPI Tutorial

Getting Started

Welcome to FlowPI! This tutorial will guide you through the setup and basic usage of our energy monitoring system. Follow these steps to get started with monitoring and optimizing your energy consumption.

1. Hardware Installation

What You'll Need

  • FlowPI Energy Monitor device
  • Power adapter (included)
  • Ethernet cable (included)
  • Current transformers (CTs) - quantity depends on your monitoring needs
  • Screwdriver and basic tools

Installation Steps

  1. Turn off power at the main breaker

    Safety first! Always ensure power is completely off before working with electrical panels.

  2. Mount the FlowPI device

    Secure the device near your electrical panel using the included mounting bracket.

  3. Install current transformers

    Clip the CT sensors around the main power lines or individual circuits you wish to monitor. Ensure the arrow on the CT points toward the load (away from the grid).

  4. Connect CTs to the FlowPI device

    Plug each CT connector into the corresponding port on the FlowPI device.

  5. Connect to network

    Use the included Ethernet cable to connect the FlowPI device to your router.

  6. Power up

    Connect the power adapter to the FlowPI device and plug it into a wall outlet.

  7. Restore main power

    Turn the main breaker back on once all connections are secure.

2. Software Setup

Creating Your Account

  1. Visit app.flowpi.com in your web browser
  2. Click on "Sign Up" and enter your email address and password
  3. Verify your email address by clicking the link sent to your inbox
  4. Log in to your new FlowPI account

Connecting Your Device

  1. In the FlowPI dashboard, click on "Add Device"
  2. Enter the device ID (found on the bottom of your FlowPI device)
  3. Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the connection
  4. Your device should now appear in your dashboard

Configuring Your Circuits

  1. Go to "Settings" > "Circuit Configuration"
  2. For each connected CT, assign a name (e.g., "Main Power", "Kitchen", "HVAC")
  3. Set the amperage rating for each circuit
  4. Save your configuration

3. Using the Dashboard

Real-Time Monitoring

The main dashboard displays your current power usage in real-time. You can see:

  • Total power consumption
  • Individual circuit usage
  • Power quality metrics
  • Cost estimates based on your utility rates

Historical Data

Access historical energy usage data to identify trends and opportunities for savings:

  • Daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly views
  • Compare periods to identify changes
  • Export data for further analysis
  • View cost breakdowns by time and circuit

Alerts & Notifications

Set up custom alerts to be notified of unusual energy usage:

  • Power usage thresholds
  • Unexpected device behavior
  • Potential electrical issues

Energy Insights

FlowPI analyzes your usage patterns to provide actionable insights:

  • Identify energy-hungry appliances
  • Receive personalized saving recommendations
  • Detect vampire power draw
  • Compare your usage to similar households

4. Advanced Features

Integration with Smart Home Systems

FlowPI works seamlessly with popular smart home platforms:

  • Home Assistant
  • SmartThings
  • Apple HomeKit
  • Google Home
  • Amazon Alexa

Visit the "Integrations" section in settings to connect your preferred platform.

API Access

For developers and advanced users, FlowPI offers a comprehensive API:

  • Access real-time and historical data
  • Control compatible smart devices
  • Create custom dashboards and applications
  • Integrate with your own systems

API documentation is available at docs.flowpi.com/api
